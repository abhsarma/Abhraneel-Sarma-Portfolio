Academic Project: Systems Approach to Design

Overview

This project explored the crucial global issue of municipal solid waste management (MSWM) and how a systems approach can be adopted in exploring a solution to this third-world problem. The management of municipal solid waste is a crippling problem in most developing countries and several developed countries. Although it is necessary for the government to implement a system for the proper collection, segregation, transfer and disposal of waste, our goal was to probe for a design intervention which could assist in overcoming this challenge at the user level.

Solution

We designed a mobile application which would allow the users to be aware of proper disposal techniques, segregation of waste in their households and make the user aware of pickup schedules (municipalty trash collection) in an attempt to create a environmentally conscious community.

Role

This project was completed in a team of two and we both collaborated throughout the design process. We independently worked on the Wireframes and the High fidelity Prototypes and then brainstormed together to create the final design.

Design Methodology

We followed a systems design methodology for this project. We began by defining the system and the various subsystems, and identified the relationships across these systems and subsystems. This model was developed from the information gathered through literature reiew. We then defined the problem for which we would design an intervention. This was followed by identifying the design goals and creating the data flow for the designed solution. Finally we embarked on creating wireframes and mockups.

Literature Research

We performed an extensive literature research in which we reviewed research papers which documented both successful and unsuccessful attempts at tackling the problem of municipal solid waste management in third world countries including India. The research helped us identify the most common causes for the various gaps that exist in MSWM. From our literature review, we made the following observations:

Poor collection in low income and low literacy communities
Integrated system required for the monitoring & management of MSW
Public awareness is essential for proper MSW management
Private contractors are more efficient in waste collection, but are expensive to municipalities
Collection in most Indian cities is very low and the government is looking into PPP framework for implementation
Proper disposal of waste after collection is required

Problem Definition

From literature analysis and the gap analysis model we understood that the problem of MSWM is multi-faceted and needs to be tackled at different levels and through different means. We have identified some of the critical aspects of the system which can be addressed through technological intervention at the user level.

Poor collection in low income and low literacy communities

Erratic collection schedules and improper disposal methods.

Integrated system required for the monitoring & management of MSW

Objective

Efficient segregation, collection of waste and promotion of recycling at the user level for ease of final disposal
