Greetings!

I am Researcher at the University of Michigan School of Information, advised by Professor Matthew Kay. I graduated from the Masters program at the University of Michigan School of Information at Ann Arbor in 2018, specialising in Human Computer Interaction and Data Science.

I am passionate about data and charts. I am interested in understanding how users interpret data presented through visualizations. This spans how users generate insiht from narrative and interactive visualizations; how communicating uncertainty in personal informatics visualizations can affect trust, decision making and behavior. I also enjoy predictive modelling of data using various statistical models, and designing new visualizations.

I am recreational football (soccer) player, lifelong Manchester United supporter, and an avid reader of books. I finished the Slaughterhouse-Five by Kurt Vonnegut, and I am currently reading Dubliners by James Joyce and Love & Math by Edward Frenkel (I don't update this section very often).
