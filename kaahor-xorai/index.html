Academic Project: Interaction Design

Overview

As part of our academic project on Interaction Design, we were given the opportunity to explore an interesting challenge: to design an intervention for a user group which is technology backward or has had extremely limited acess to technology. Through literature research the user group that we identified were the brass and bell metal workers of Hajo. These workers were engaged in the state of Assam's traditional metal-craft industry which produces items for both everyday and decorative uses. The industry which is almost exclusively confined to two districts in Assam has fallen into almost near demise due to several factors.

Role

We worked in a team of three members for the project. All three members were completely involved through the entire process of the project, although responsibilities were divided through the different stages of the project. I was primarily responsible for the wireframes and high-fidelity mockups.

Project Details

Objective

To research on the brass and bell metal workers in Hajo, an user group which is technologically backward; and design an intervention to aid them in their occupation.

Methodology

We employed a user centred design methodology, as described below, for this project.

Literature Research and gap analysis to identify a potential user group and understanding the current scenario.

Contextual Enquiry by visiting the workers in Hajo and observing and interacting with them.

Affinity Analysis of the the information gathered from literature review and user interviews.

Persona building to understand the needs and motivations of the users.

Problem Identification and Brainstorming on possible solutions to create an intervention.

Design of a solution to provide better producer-seller linkage in the consumer chain.

User Research

For our user research we surveyed the region extensively trying to identify the different facilities that are present in the area such as transport and communication, education, healthcare etc. We conducted 12 user interviews: 2 unstructured and 10 structured. No screening was performed before conducting interviews. The interviewees varied in their monthly income and thus resulted in some differing opinions. However, we found that almost all the interviewees echoed similar sentiments.
The conclusions that we were able to draw from the user interviews have been listed below:

The household nature of the industry and the lack of formal training confines the scale of activities to individual household.
Price of raw material is increasing more rapidly than the selling price causing a decline of the industry.
Lack of demand outside Assam, due to the coarse nature of our work. Fine quality products with intricate engravings, which are more in demand are really time-consuming and require a lot of skill.
Competition from industries outside Assam which uses machines to produce cheaper and better finished products.

Affinity Analysis

We grouped the users into three broad categories and based on the observations and documentation from user interviews, we performed an affinity analysis of the data.

1

One or Two working members in the family and belonged to the very poor category.

Dependent on middlemen for resources.

Didn’t have access to healthcare and schools for themselves and their families.

2

One or Two working members in the family, they were relatively better off as compared to the first category.

They acquire raw materials from mahajans or middlemen on loan

3

Three or more working members in the family with a quite well established business.

Mostly concerned with a lack of government aid to this traditional industry and a crippling lack of demand of goods from both within and outside the state

Solution

From the analysis it was clear that one of the reasons why this industry has been struggling is the lack of a producer-seller network. Another problem that these workers often face is the exploitation by middlemen and sellers.

We designed a platform which will connect the producers to sellers directly and create a transparent network for distribution. As the industry is confined to two districts with decent land connectivity, the development of a goods transportation network to major Indian cities would not be a problem. We decided to name this platform Kaahor Xorai which is the Assamese traditional offering tray used to greet elders or guests.

Design Considerations

Keeping in mind the user group and their limitations in interacting with technology and other limitations imposed because of the culture, social and economic conditions, we determined the following constraints for our design:

Poor Connectivity

Provide information on how to deliver an order.

Database of transport companies.

Slow Internet, thus less content, minimal and flat UI, and contact option.

Lengthy Manufacturing Process

Demand before supply.

Good notification system.

Connecting the buyers and designers with the producers.

Ask to specify delivery time.

Provide buyers contact information for security.

Technologically Backward

Simple and easy to use Interface.

Limited Functions.

Tutorial system should be present and intuitive elements should be used.

Use of viusal elements, Intuitive interface and lesser no. of tasks.

Other Constraints

Lack of Devices: The target audience are financial burdened. Hence the solution should be Cheap tablet compatible.

Language constraints: The target audience is primarily familiar with Assamese only, hence the solution should have multi-lingual compatibility.
